Respectful Online Communication

  • ü§ù Treat Others with Kindness: Use the same respect online that you would show in person. Remember there's a real person behind every screen.
  • üõë Stop Cyberbullying: Don't participate in or share mean comments, embarrassing photos, or hurtful messages about others.
  • üí≠ Think Before You Type: Read your message twice before sending. Ask yourself: "Would I say this face-to-face?"

Recognizing Phishing & Scams

Learn to identify and avoid online scams that try to steal your personal information or money.

  • üìß Suspicious Emails: Be wary of emails asking for passwords, personal info, or money ‚Äì especially if they create urgency or fear.
  • üîó Check Links Carefully: Hover over links to see the real URL before clicking. Be suspicious of shortened links from unknown sources.
  • üí∞ Too Good to Be True: Free money, prizes, or "you've won" messages are usually scams. If it sounds too good to be true, it probably is.
  • üìû Verify Requests: If someone claims to be from your bank, school, or a company, call them directly using official phone numbers to verify.
  • üö® Red Flags: Poor grammar, urgent deadlines, requests for gift cards, or threats are all warning signs of scams.

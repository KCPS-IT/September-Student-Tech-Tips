Online Safety & Privacy

Protect yourself and your personal information while navigating the digital world.

  • 🔒 Use Strong Passwords: Create unique passwords with a mix of letters, numbers, and symbols. Never share your passwords with anyone.
  • 👥 Think Before You Share: Don't post personal information like your address, phone number, or school schedule on social media.
  • ⚙️ Check Privacy Settings: Regularly review and update privacy settings on all your social media accounts and apps.
  • 📱 Be App Smart: Only download apps from official app stores and read permissions before installing.
  • 🌐 Secure Browsing: Look for "https://" and the lock icon when entering personal information on websites.
⚠️
Remember: Once something is posted online, it can be there forever. Think twice before posting photos, comments, or personal information.
💬

Respectful Online Communication

Build positive digital relationships and create a better online environment for everyone.

  • 🤝 Treat Others with Kindness: Use the same respect online that you would show in person. Remember there's a real person behind every screen.
  • 🛑 Stop Cyberbullying: Don't participate in or share mean comments, embarrassing photos, or hurtful messages about others.
  • 💭 Think Before You Type: Read your message twice before sending. Ask yourself: "Would I say this face-to-face?"
  • 🗣️ Speak Up Safely: If you see cyberbullying or inappropriate behavior, report it to a trusted adult or use the platform's reporting features.
  • 🎯 Stay on Topic: Keep conversations relevant and avoid spam or off-topic messages that might annoy others.
💡
Golden Rule: Treat others online the way you want to be treated. Your words have power – use them to build up, not tear down.
🎣

Recognizing Phishing & Scams

Learn to identify and avoid online scams that try to steal your personal information or money.

  • 📧 Suspicious Emails: Be wary of emails asking for passwords, personal info, or money – especially if they create urgency or fear.
  • 🔗 Check Links Carefully: Hover over links to see the real URL before clicking. Be suspicious of shortened links from unknown sources.
  • 💰 Too Good to Be True: Free money, prizes, or "you've won" messages are usually scams. If it sounds too good to be true, it probably is.
  • 📞 Verify Requests: If someone claims to be from your bank, school, or a company, call them directly using official phone numbers to verify.
  • 🚨 Red Flags: Poor grammar, urgent deadlines, requests for gift cards, or threats are all warning signs of scams.
🚨
When in Doubt: Don't click, don't download, don't share personal information. Ask a trusted adult, teacher, or parent for help identifying suspicious messages.
